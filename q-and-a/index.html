Q1: What does BCNN stand for?
BCNN stands for integrity in the world of journalism. It stands for ethical business practices anywhere business is conducted. It stands for the just treatment of workers in all workforces. It stands for freedom of information everywhere it's needed. Most importantly, BCNN stands for you. To learn more about what BCNN stands for, visit our "about us" page.
Q2: How can I get ahold of someone at BCNN?
Please visit our "Contact Us" page for ways to get ahold of us.
Q3: Where can I find the latest BCNN stories?
You can find the latest stories on the "Happening NOW" page. It will load the latest stories based on how recently they were uploaded to BCNN (which may or may not correspond to their listed publishing date). This page can be found by clicking on the "More" button at the top right, which brings up various important pages. Latest stories can also be found on the home page.
Alternatively, you can go to the "Find a Story" page, leave the title blank and the topic as "all" when you search, and scroll to the very bottom to find the most recently published story.
Q4: How can I find a specific story/topic/source/area of coverage?
Individual stories can be searched for on the "Find a Story" page. Topics can be searched for on the "Topics" page. Specific sources can be searched for on the "Sources" page. Stories grouped by covering similar subjects, or areas of coverage, can be found and/or searched for on the "Need to Know" page.
